Clash of Clans Time Tracker

O.T.T.O. Progress Tracker

40+ users report the tool was helpful in completing the O.T.T.O. Challenges and over 250 players used the tool in 2022.

Visit the O.T.T.O. Tool
What's the problem?

I've been playing Clash of Clans since its release over ten years ago, and I must admit, it makes me feel super old. One significant addition to the game was the introduction of the Builder Base in October 17. Supercell eventually added an additional builder who could travel between both bases. This was a game-changer because builders are incredibly valuable and having an extra one allows players to progress through the game a whopping 20% faster.
However, the journey to unlock the next builder through the builder base was quite an arduous one. Many players found it long, boring, and downright exhausting. It wasn't exactly the most enjoyable aspect of the game. Despite that, some of us realized that the reward of unlocking the sixth builder for free made it all worth it. It required patience and dedication, but in the end, the benefits outweighed the tedium.

To obtain the O.T.T.O. hut, I had to go through a series of steps in my home base. First, I had to "gear up" three buildings, upgrade the cannon cart to level 18, the mega tesla to level 9, and the battlemachine to level 30. At first glance, it didn't seem too challenging, but, was I wrong.

The real struggle came from the scarcity of resources in the builder base. Sure, there were a few collectors to contribute to my progress, but the bulk of the resources had to be earned through victorious attacks. To maximize my resource gain, I had to win three attacks every day. The rewards from battles varied depending on the trophy count of my opponents. This meant that I had to actively engage in the game and constantly strive to improve my skills to progress through the base effectively. It required dedication and active play to keep the resources flowing.

In Clash of Clans, planning plays a crucial role in ensuring efficient resource management and optimal use of builders. Many players, including myself, rely on tools like Clash Ninja to plan and strategize the allocation of our resources. However, I noticed that Clash Ninja lacked a robust system for tracking the completion progress of the builder base. This realization motivated me to take matters into my own hands and develop a tool that would provide a clear understanding of how much further I needed to progress in order to unlock the desired features.

So what can I do about it?

During that time, I was a Junior in college, and I had already taken several classes that taught us how to utilize Excel effectively. It was in line with this website project that I embarked on a journey to gain a deeper understanding of various tools that I would encounter in my future endeavors. I decided to challenge myself by creating a calculator specifically designed to estimate the time required for completing the builder hut in Clash of Clans. The Clash of Clans Wiki. To start off, I began gathering the necessary data from a community website called Clash of Clans Wiki. This valuable resource provided comprehensive data tables containing the cost and duration for each upgrade in the game. Armed with this information, I was ready to dive into the development process of my calculator.

A significant portion of my time was dedicated to cleaning the data I gathered and ensuring that the values were correctly set to their respective units. Time, for instance, had different formats depending on the duration of the upgrades. Shorter upgrades were measured in minutes, while longer ones required a combination of days and hours.

To create a user-friendly interface, I utilized Excel's VLookup tools to implement dropdown menus for users to select their current upgrade levels. This system also enabled me to reference the data tables and incorporate them into the formulas. This aspect of the project was the most challenging and required thoughtful consideration. Once players made their selections, various cells would dynamically change, displaying information such as the next upgrade's cost, the time required to complete it, and the cumulative time and resources necessary to upgrade the building to the required level for the challenge.

What research did you do?

I wanted this project to be community-driven because I recognized that I was not alone in my frustration. I believed that empowering players with a better understanding of the time commitment required for the challenge would help them overcome it. The frustration stemmed not only from the challenge itself but primarily from the uncertainty surrounding its duration. One player aptly compared it to the frustration of waiting in an emergency room without knowing how long it will take to be seen, and described it as an agonizing experience.

Who uses this tool?

In my quest to create a useful tool for calculating the time needed to complete the builder hut challenge in Clash of Clans, I reached out to the Clash of Clans Reddit community for their valuable insights. I wanted to understand why the challenge frustrated them, what features they desired in a time calculation tool, and whether such a tool was even necessary. After a week of working on the project, I felt it was ready for user testing, although not yet ready for an official release. I managed to gather a group of seven individuals who were willing to test the tool, and the results were mixed. Some players raised concerns, suggesting that I might be reinventing the wheel or even attempting to copy Clash Ninja. To address these concerns and ensure I wasn't stepping on anyone's toes, I reached out to them on Discord and engaged in a conversation about my project. I reassured them that my intentions were solely to develop my own tool and that their website had inspired me to do so. To my relief, they were excited to hear about my project and supported its development.

With the valuable feedback received from the testing phase, I felt confident enough to release a full version of the tool. I published it on Reddit, and once again, the reception was mixed. However, I took the opportunity to explain the intentions and specific use of the tool to the community. After understanding its purpose, people began to appreciate and receive the tool well.

What lessons were learned?

In total, over 250 people used the tool. Several people thanked me for the work that I put into the tool because they were able to motivate themselves to finish it because they didn't realize how close they were. It was a difficult but fun personal project that allowed me to understand the importance of market research. One of my favorite topics in Economics is that of externalities. Externalities, both positive and negative, can show us our effectiveness in planning. Some are unavoidable, but, some aren't and can be addressed with proper planning. The lesson from this project is to always conduct research before making a major decision. Engagning with others provides additional perspectives to the difficulties that could lie ahead.

Additionally, I made sure that this project was not done the 'easy way.' I made this project more difficult by using VLookup tables, dropdown lists, and multiple sheets which made it difficult to transfer between at times. I wanted to learn Excel, so, I found the topics that are used most in Financial Analysis. It said that Vlookup, conditional formatting with an added key, connections between sheets, indexes and matches, sumif and sumifs, etc. This resource uses all of those with labeling for the important cells. This provides the optimal user experience because they don't have to navigate a bulky excel file. But, it doesn't let them go too quickly to ensure they don't miss any of the necessary input boxes.
